# A/B Test Analysis Findings

## Executive Summary

- Control users: 3,000
- Treatment users: 3,000
- Control purchase rate: 14.33%
- Treatment purchase rate: 18.47%
- Absolute uplift: 4.13 percentage points
- Relative uplift: 28.84%
- P-value: 0.00002
- Statistically significant at 5%: Yes

## Key Findings

1. The treatment increased purchase conversion from **14.33%** to **18.47%**.
2. Revenue per user moved from **$16.26** in control to **$21.58** in treatment.
3. The strongest positive device response came from **Tablet**, with a purchase-rate uplift of **7.03** points.
4. The weakest segment response came from **Paid Search**, with a purchase-rate uplift of **0.10** points.

## Recommendation

Roll out the treatment if the business prioritizes purchase conversion and revenue per user, while monitoring weaker-response segments after launch. Pair the rollout with follow-up analysis on traffic sources that underperform relative to the average treatment effect.

## How To Present This In Interviews

Explain the primary metric, show the control-versus-treatment lift, mention the significance test, and close with why segment-level analysis is critical before a full rollout decision.
